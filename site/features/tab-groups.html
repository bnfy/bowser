home/features/tab groups

a little order

Keep the tabs you need. Tuck away the rest.

Name a group for the task in front of you. Blanc keeps that group on the Island and leaves the rest close, not constantly visible.

keep work in context

Groups have names, not colors.

Name the task, then keep moving.

Use /group work to create or join a named group from the active tab. A group exists only while it has tabs in it.

Keep the active group close.

The Island shows the active group's tabs and name. Other groups live in the expanded panel until you choose to switch back to them.

Fold the rest without losing the thread.

Groups can tuck away in the panel, and the Quick Switcher finds them by name when it is time to return.

fewer things asking for attention

A group is a focus tool, not another layer of browser chrome.

Blanc gives the active task a quiet place in the Island while keeping the rest of your session nearby. Pinned tabs stay with their group, and a plain new tab starts ungrouped.
